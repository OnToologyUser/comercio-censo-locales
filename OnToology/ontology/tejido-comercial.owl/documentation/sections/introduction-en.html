Vocabulary for the representation of data related to the census of business premises and terraces of a municipality, as well as their associated economic activities and opening licenses. This vocabulary has been developed in the context of the project "Plataforma de gobierno abierto, colaborativa e interoperable" (Open Government collaborative and interoperable platform), where the municipalities of Madrid, Zaragoza, Santiago de Compostela and A Coru√±a are involved. It reuses terms from the [schema.org](http://schema.org/) vocabulary. Furthermore, it refers to terms from other vocabularies used for the representation of concepts in domains such as geographical location, administrative territorial units, postal addresses and streets. More specifically, it uses the [geosparql](http://www.opengis.net/ont/geosparql#) ontology and several terms from the [wgs84](http://www.w3.org/2003/01/geo/wgs84_pos#) and [geo_core](https://datos.ign.es/def/geo_core#) ontologies to represent the geographical location of businesses, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. This follows a usual pattern for the representation of the geospatial location of items in a municipality. It also uses the [territory](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/sector-publico/territorio#) vocabulary to describe territorial entities in Spain, such as census tract, district, neighbourhood, etc., where the business premise is located in. It also reuses the [postal address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/direccion-postal#) vocabulary to describe the postal address of the business premise, if available. Finally, it also uses the [streets](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/callejero#) vocabulary to represent the details of the street where the business premise is located in.
